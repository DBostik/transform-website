If so, then there’s no better time than now to open up an afternoon for yourself and head to the nearest bike shop to get a basic idea of which style of road bike is right for you.

When you go to a bike shop that specializes in the many different elements road bike’s can be equipped with, it’s likely a knowledgeable staff member will be able to assist you with what you are looking for and may offer you to take a test ride.

It’s a good idea to ask whoever is helping you, if they will take measurements and match you to a bike that will be most comfortable for your body type. This will make a big difference in how much you will take your new road bike out for a spin because you will be more apt to ride a bike that conforms to your unique frame.

Before spending a significant amount of money on a new road bike, you will need to also consider what you will be using it for. Will you be riding on rough terrain? Commuting back and forth through the city? Are you aspiring to beat your Chicago personal trainer in a biking marathon?

Determine where you will be riding and if you will need to carry belongings along the way when you ride so you can choose a bicycle that will suit your lifestyle.

The type of road bike frame and the quality of it’s components are more technical aspects of making this type of purchase. It will be necessary to consider what the frame is made from if you are looking for something that will allow you to have the most comfortable ride.

Aluminum framed bikes usually weigh less and are absolutely more sustainable and efficient, but is also not as comfortable as a steel frame. A road bike made from carbon fibers are top performers and often offer the most comfort however, they can be very pricey.

When making a road bike purchase, it’s also important to pay attention to its wheels. The wheels of your bicycle will impact how the ride feels considerably.  Be sure to ask the bike shop attendant which wheels are recommended for your specific activity.

A top Chicago personal trainer states, “Before you make a pricey purchase, stand over the frame of the bike to determine how far off of the ground you feel. Ask the bike shop attendant to adjust the seat and handlebars if needed and also if you can take it for a test ride.

Make sure you get the fit perfected before you head back home with an expensive road bike that is not comfortable for you to ride, because it’s likely you won’t ride an uncomfortable bicycle.”

There are lots of different types available today from road bikes designed for long distances to hybrid bikes that are suited for pedaling uphill with many different paint colors and brands to choose from.  Purchasing a road bike is a great investment for living a health conscious life and a great way to get you excited about fitness.
